01 · About

Who I am

Systems administrator based in Launceston, Tasmania. Most of my time is spent on Linux and macOS — automating repetitive work, building practical tools for other sysadmins, and keeping servers running properly.

Everything I publish is something I genuinely use myself. The cleanup scripts came from being sick of repeating the same setup process on every new machine. The sysadmin knife exists because I wanted one tool instead of a dozen tabs. The POE marketer happened because spreadsheets stopped being interesting.

I also run and facilitate online communities. Over the years, I've moderated and administered multiple intentional communities, some with more than 50,000 members, before eventually building a space called Home for the people who wanted the same kind of environment I did. The idea is simple: people should be able to have fun without walking on eggshells. No overbearing moderation, no language policing, no manufactured outrage. Around half the community is neurodivergent or LGBTQ+, and that's never been treated as unusual — it's just people being people. I'm open to everyone except genuinely hateful people, though I still think some people can change. Politically, I sit somewhere in the middle and lean more on common sense than ideology. I care about people far more than being performatively correct.

I'll never pretend to understand something I don't. If I don't know something properly, I'll say it directly. And when I come across someone with a massive ego, I'm usually happy to let them keep talking.

Outside of work, I spend time in Home on Discord and build tools and writing workflows around my offline WorkSafe AI setup — my way of getting something useful out of modern AI without shipping every thought and document off to someone else's servers.
